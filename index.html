RESEARCH
My current astrophysics research interests involve galaxy-scale numerical simulations. I am working to understand the formation and evolution of the Magellanic Stream, a huge gaseous structure covering 200 degrees along the sky. The gas in this Stream originated in the Large and Small Magellanic Clouds, two dwarf galaxy satellites of our own Galaxy, the Milky Way. The Magellanic Clouds have been orbiting around each other for several billion years and throughout these interactions the majority of thier gas has been stripped out into the Magellanic Stream. The header image at the top of the page shows how the gas in the Magellanic Stream would appear in the night sky if we could see radio waves. Recently, we have shown that the inclusion of a warm circumgalactic medium around the LMC (motivated by its high mass) can account for the even more massive amount of ionized gas surrounding the neutral hydrogen Stream that we can see in emission.
